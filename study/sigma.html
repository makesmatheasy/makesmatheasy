Sigma Notation



Ʃ is a letter of greek alphabets and it is called sigma. The symbol sigma (Ʃ) represents the sum of similar terms.

Properties of Sigma Notation

\[1.) \sum_{r = 1}^{n} T_r \,=\,T1\,+\,T2\,+\,T3\,+....+\,T_n\, , \, when \, T_n \, is \, the \, general \, term \, of \, the \, series \]

\[2.) \sum_{r = 1}^{n} \, ( \, T_r ± T_r^{'} \, ) \,= \, \sum_{r = 1}^{n} T_r \, ± \, \sum_{r = 1}^{n} T_r^{'} \, [ sigma \, operator \, is \, distributive \, over \, addition \, and \, subtraction ] \]

\[3.) \sum_{r = 1}^{n} T_r T_r^{'} \, != \, ( \, \sum_{r = 1}^{n} T_r \, ) \, ( \, \sum_{r = 1}^{n} T_r^{'} \, ) \, [ \, sigma \, operator \, is \, not \, distributive \, over \, multiplication \, ] \]

\[4.) \sum_{r = 1}^{n} \frac{T_r}{T_r^{'}} \, != \, \frac{(\sum_{r = 1}^{n} T_r)}{(\sum_{r = 1}^{n} T_r^{'})} \, [ \, sigma \, operator \, is \, not \, distributive \, over \, division \, ] \]

\[5.) \sum_{r = 1}^{n} a \, T_r \, = \, a \, \sum_{r = 1}^{n} T_r \, [ \, where \, a \, is \, constant \, ] \]

\[6.) \sum_{j = 1}^{n} \sum_{i = 1}^{n} T_i T_j \, = \, ( \, \sum_{i = 1}^{n} T_i \, ) \, ( \, \sum_{j = 1}^{n} T_j \, ) \, [ \, where \, i \, and \, j \, are \, independent \, ] \]
