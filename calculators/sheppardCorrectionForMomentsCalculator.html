Sheppard's correction for moments Calculator

Sheppard’s Correction for Grouping Errors are adjustments to calculated sample moments for grouped data (i.e. data that has been binned). The correction is named after W.F. Shepard, who noted than when moments are calculated for continuous frequency distributions, it is assumed that the data is centered around the class interval midpoints. This erroneous assumption introduces “grouping errors” in calculation of moments. Grouping errors only happen in even moments (e.g. second and fourth moments), so the correction isn’t needed for odd-numbered moments.
