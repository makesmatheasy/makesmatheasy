Sensitivity and Specificity



Sensitivity


Sensitivity is the ability of a test to correctly classify an individual as ′diseased′


Sensitivity = a / a+c = a (true positive) / a+c (true positive + false negative) = Probability of being test positive when disease present.



Specificity


The ability of a test to correctly classify an individual as disease- free is called the test′s specificity.


Specificity = d / b+d = d (true negative) / b+d (true negative + false positive) = Probability of being test negative when disease absent.
